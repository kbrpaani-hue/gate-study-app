Phase 1 · Jul 21 – Aug 22 (5 weeks) · Full syllabus, first pass

By Aug 22, you'll have touched every ECE topic at least once. That's the milestone — not mastery. Mastery is what Phases 2–4 are for.

Phase 2 – 4 · Aug 23 → Feb 2027

Phase 2 — Subject-wise Mastery
Aug 23 – Oct 11  ·  7 weeks
›

Rotate through subjects in weightage order (Maths → Networks → Signals → EDC → Analog → Control → Comms → Digital → EMTL), spending ~4-5 days per subject. Weekday nights: solve 15-20 PYQs on that subject. Weekends: one subject-wise mini-mock (30 Qs, timed) + redo every wrong answer. No new topics — only PYQs, standard problems, and building speed. Start a formula-sheet-per-subject if you haven't already.

Phase 3 — Full Mock Test Phase
Oct 12 – Dec 6  ·  8 weeks
›

Full 65-question, 3-hour timed mock every Sunday (Arihant's mock/PYQ paper sets). Monday = pure error analysis: for every wrong question, figure out if it was a concept gap, a silly mistake, or a time-pressure mistake — they need different fixes. Weeknights: fix the top 3 weak topics surfaced by that week's mock. Add 30 min/day of GA — it's 15 free marks and compounds fast.

Phase 4 — Final Sprint
Dec 7 – Feb 5  ·  9 weeks
›

Dec 7 – Jan 10: mock every 3-4 days, alternating with formula-sheet revision — no new topics, ever. Jan 11 – Jan 25: light revision only, 4-5 hrs/day max, sleep protected. Jan 26 – Feb 4: formula sheets only, 1-2 hrs/day, rest. Feb 5: complete rest, check admit card/documents, sleep by 10 PM. Exam day: execute, don't panic on one hard question — mark and move on.

📚 Using the Arihant Guide Efficiently
  • Don't read every derivation in full — read theory once, then jump straight to solved examples
  • Mark every topic you find genuinely hard with a star — that's your Phase 2 priority list
  • Use Arihant's own PYQ sections at the end of each chapter for weekend practice
⚡ Rules for Learning 5 New Topics a Day
  • Day-1 exposure is not mastery — you're building a map, not memorizing the terrain
  • If a topic doesn't click in the morning read, don't stall — move on, it'll make more sense after solving examples that night
  • Never skip the weekend PYQ block to "get ahead" on new topics — repetition is what actually builds the score
  • Negative marking: −1/3 for MCQ, 0 for NAT — attempt every NAT, be selective on MCQs you're unsure of
🎯 Realistic Score Ladder
  • 45–55 → Solid outcome for a zero-base 6-month sprint alongside college — decent NIT range
  • 55–65 → Very strong for this timeline — good NITs, some IITs
  • 65–75 → Excellent execution — top NITs, several IITs, PSU-eligible
  • 75+ → Exceptional — would put you well ahead of the average full-timeline aspirant
  • 90 stays out of reach in this timeline — but hitting 65-75 from zero in 6 months while in college is genuinely something to be proud of
🏋️ Don't Skip This
  • Sleep is non-negotiable — 6+ hrs on weekdays, this is when the day's learning actually consolidates
  • Burnout around month 3-4 kills more GATE attempts than difficulty does — the Sunday evening free-time block exists on purpose, protect it
  • If you fall behind on Phase 1's schedule by a few days, that's fine — the buffer days at the end exist for exactly that
